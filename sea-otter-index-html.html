Sea Otters: Charming Guardians of Kelp Forests

Discover the fascinating world of these playful marine mammals and their crucial role in coastal ecosystems.

About Sea Otters

Sea otters (Enhydra lutris) are marine mammals native to the coasts of the northern and eastern North Pacific Ocean. As members of the weasel family, they are the heaviest mustelids, with adults typically weighing between 14 and 45 kg (30 to 100 lb).

Unlike most marine mammals, sea otters rely on their exceptionally thick fur for insulation rather than blubber. This fur is the densest in the animal kingdom, with up to 1 million hairs per square inch. While they can walk on land, sea otters are most at home in the water, where they spend most of their time.

These charismatic creatures are known for their playful behavior, tool use, and crucial role in maintaining the health of kelp forest ecosystems. By controlling sea urchin populations, sea otters help preserve these underwater forests, which provide habitat for countless marine species.

Prey & Predators

Prey

  • Sea Urchins
  • Crabs
  • Clams
  • Mussels
  • Octopuses
  • Fish

Predators

  • Orcas (Killer Whales)
  • Great White Sharks
  • Bald Eagles (preying on pups)
  • Humans (historically for fur trade)
